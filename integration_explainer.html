Key concepts

What is a Riemann sum?

A Riemann sum approximates the area under a curve by dividing the interval into n strips, drawing a rectangle in each strip, and summing their areas: Σ f(xᵢ) · Δx. The sample point xᵢ can be the left edge, right edge, or midpoint of each strip.

What is a definite integral?

The definite integral ∫ₐᵇ f(x) dx is the limit of the Riemann sum as the number of rectangles approaches infinity (and their width approaches zero). It gives the exact area under the curve from a to b.

Why does the method (Left / Mid / Right) matter?

Each method places the sample point differently. For a monotonically increasing function, Left always underestimates and Right always overestimates. Midpoint is generally the most accurate. But all three converge to the same value as n → ∞ — which is why the integral is well-defined regardless of how you choose sample points.

The limit definition

∫ₐᵇ f(x) dx = lim(n→∞) Σᵢ f(xᵢ) · Δx, where Δx = (b−a)/n. As n → ∞, Δx → 0, and the staircase of rectangles perfectly traces the smooth curve. This is the bridge between discrete summation and continuous integration.
